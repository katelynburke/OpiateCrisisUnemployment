## The Opioid Crisis and the Impact on Unemployment in the United States

By Rishi Hebbar, Katelyn Burke, Xuan He, and Ashraf Al Mamun

### Background Information

According to the National Institute on Drug Abuse, more than 130 people in the United States die each day from an opiod overdose. The opiod crisis has become more severe in the U.S. year after year. The midwestern region saw opioid overdoses increase 70 percent from July 2016 through September 2017.
